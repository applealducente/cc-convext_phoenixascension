Opening Spiel

Opening Spiel

What you say in the first 10 seconds. Confident, warm, and quick — get to the reason for the call before they decide to hang up.

"Hi, is this [Customer Name]? Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] calling from Homeaglow — you signed up recently for a home cleaning, just calling to get that scheduled for you. Do you have a quick minute?"

If they sound rushed: "No problem at all — this'll take less than two minutes, I just need to confirm a few details about your home and get you on the calendar."

Goals of this phase

  • Confirm identity and that they remember signing up
  • Set a low-pressure, helpful tone
  • Transition smoothly into Discovery

Discovery

Learn about the home and the customer's situation before pitching anything. This is where you gather the details the Cleaning Calculator needs.

"To get you matched with the right cleaner and the right plan, can you tell me a bit about your place — how many bedrooms and bathrooms are we working with?"

"And is there anything specific you're hoping to get taken care of — deep cleaning, certain rooms, pets in the home, anything like that?"

What to capture

  • Bedrooms / bathrooms
  • Pets, allergies, special requests
  • Preferred days/times
  • Any areas needing extra attention (deep clean candidates)

Elevator Pitch

A 20-30 second summary of the value — used after Discovery, before diving into details.

"Here's how it works: we match you with a vetted, background-checked cleaner who comes on a schedule that works for you. You're covered by our satisfaction guarantee, so if anything's ever not right, we make it right — no extra charge. Membership also includes [benefits], and you can pause or adjust anytime."

Data Gathering

Before quoting a price, confirm the details that drive the estimate.

"Just to make sure I quote this accurately — does your home have any pets, how would you describe the current condition (lived-in, needs a deep clean, pretty tidy), and roughly what size is the space in square footage?"

Checklist

  • Bedrooms & bathrooms (for hour estimate)
  • Current condition of home
  • Pets / special surfaces
  • Preferred frequency (1x–4x per month)
  • Preferred hours / availability

Use the Cleaning Calculator tool once you have these details to generate an estimate.

Sales Pitch

Present the estimate and membership value clearly. Lead with the plan, then the price, then the guarantee.

"Based on what you've told me, I'd recommend starting with [X] hours per visit, [frequency]x a month. With the $59/month membership, that comes out to about $[estimate] per visit. That membership also covers [benefits] — and every cleaning is backed by our happiness guarantee."

Tips

  • Anchor on the monthly value, not just the per-visit cost
  • Mention the guarantee before they ask
  • Pause after stating the price — let them respond

Booking

Move from "yes" to a scheduled appointment without friction.

"Great — let's get this on the calendar. Looking at your preferred availability, I have [date/time options]. Which works best for you?"

If hesitant: "No commitment needed beyond the first cleaning — if it's not a fit, you can cancel anytime. Let's just get the first one scheduled so you can see how it goes."

Booking Proper

Confirming all details before ending the call.

"Perfect — to confirm, I've got you booked for [date] at [time], for a [X]-hour cleaning. You'll get a confirmation by text/email with your cleaner's details. Is the address on file still [address]?"

Before hanging up, confirm:

  • Date, time, and duration
  • Address and entry instructions
  • Payment method on file
  • Membership terms (frequency, fee, cancellation policy)

Verbatim

Exact phrasing for sensitive or commonly mishandled moments.

Cancellation policy: "You're free to cancel or pause anytime through your account — there's no long-term lock-in on the standard plan."

Guarantee: "If you're ever not happy with a cleaning, let us know within 24 hours and we'll send someone back out to make it right, free of charge."

Payment: "You're only charged after each cleaning is completed — nothing is billed upfront."

Value Statements

Reusable lines that reinforce why the service is worth it — drop these in naturally throughout the call.

  • "Every cleaner is background-checked and vetted before they're matched with customers."
  • "You get the same trusted cleaner whenever possible, so they learn your home and preferences."
  • "Membership pricing is lower than booking one-off cleanings every time."
  • "Our guarantee means you're never stuck with a cleaning you're not happy with."
  • "You're in control — adjust frequency, hours, or pause anytime from your account."

Common Objections

Standard pushback and how to respond.

"It's too expensive."

"I understand — totally fair. A lot of customers feel that way at first, but when you break it down, it's about $[X] per hour, which is in line with hiring an independent cleaner, except you get the guarantee, vetting, and flexibility on top. Would it help if we adjusted the hours or frequency to fit your budget better?"

"I want to think about it."

"Of course — no pressure at all. Can I ask what's giving you pause, just so I can make sure you have everything you need to decide? [Address concern.] If it helps, we can book the first cleaning now with no obligation — you can always cancel before it happens if you change your mind."

"I'm worried about a stranger in my home."

"That's completely understandable, and it's something we take seriously. Every cleaner goes through a background check before they're matched with customers, and you'll get their name and profile ahead of time so you know exactly who's coming."

"I already have a cleaner."

"That's great that you have someone you trust! Out of curiosity, is there anything about your current setup you'd change if you could — pricing, reliability, flexibility? [Tailor pitch to gap.]"

"I don't want a subscription / membership."

"Totally get it — if a recurring plan isn't the right fit, we do have a one-time cleaning option as well. Want me to run those numbers for you instead?"

Discovery Questions

Open-ended questions to understand needs and build rapport.

  • "What made you decide to look into a cleaning service?"
  • "Have you used a cleaning service before? What did you like or not like about it?"
  • "How would you describe the day-to-day in your home — busy household, pets, kids?"
  • "Are there any areas of the home that tend to need the most attention?"
  • "What does your ideal cleaning schedule look like?"
  • "Is there anything that would make this an easy yes for you today?"

FAQs

How is pricing determined?

Pricing is based on the number of hours needed per cleaning, which depends on home size, condition, and frequency selected. Membership fee is a flat monthly add-on.

Can the customer change their schedule later?

Yes — frequency, hours, and timing can all be adjusted from the account after the first cleaning.

What happens if they cancel early?

Depending on the cohort/plan, an Early Termination Fee (ETF) may apply if cancelling before the minimum commitment is met. Use the ETF Calculator to estimate this.

Do they need to be home during the cleaning?

No — many customers provide entry instructions (lockbox, code, key) and aren't home during the visit.

What's included in the membership fee?

The membership fee covers account management, the satisfaction guarantee, rebooking support, and access to the cleaner network — it's separate from the per-visit cleaning cost.

User Manual

  2. Move to Discovery to understand the customer's needs.
  3. Use Data Gathering to collect required fields in your CRM.
  4. Present the appropriate Sales Pitch based on plan type.
  5. Complete Booking and confirm with Booking Proper.
  6. Read Verbatim scripts exactly as written when required.
  7. Use Value Statements and Common Objections to handle pushback.
  8. Refer to Discovery Questions and FAQs as needed throughout the call.
  9. Use the calculator tools on the right toolbar for pricing, ETF, one-time, and negotiation quotes.
