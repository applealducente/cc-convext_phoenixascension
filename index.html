Opening Spiel

Phase 1: Opening Spiel

These leads have gone quiet for a few weeks. They likely don't remember the original call, may not recognize the number, and could be wary of "another sales call." Keep it short, warm, and give them an easy reason to stay on the line — lead with curiosity or a quick win, not a recap of the past.

Opener A — The Quick Favor / Curiosity Hook

"Hi, is this (Customer Name)? Hey, this is (Your Name) with Homeaglow — quick one for you. A little while back you'd looked into getting your place cleaned, and I just wanted to check in because we've actually got some new pricing that's a lot more affordable now. Got like 20 seconds?"

Opener B — The "Still Interested?" Hook

"Hey (Customer Name), this is (Your Name) calling from Homeaglow. I know it's been a bit since we last spoke — I'm just doing a quick round of calls to folks who checked us out before to see if a home cleaning is still something you'd want, now that we've got some better offers running. Is that something you'd still be open to?"

Opener C — The Direct Value Lead

"Hi (Customer Name), (Your Name) here from Homeaglow. I'll keep this quick — we're running a promo where your first cleaning can be as low as $9, and I wanted to make sure you didn't miss it since you'd looked into this before. Want me to walk you through it real quick?"

If they sound annoyed or say "I already told you no"

"Totally fair, I appreciate you letting me know. Just so I'm not wasting your time — was it more about the price, or just not the right time back then? If it's pricing, the offers have actually changed quite a bit since we last spoke, and it might be worth a quick look."

Goals of this phase

  • Re-engage without sounding like a repeat cold call — lead with what's new
  • Get a small "yes" (20 seconds, quick question) before pitching anything
  • If they push back immediately, pivot to a one-line value hook instead of re-explaining the whole service

Onset Objections

These are the immediate, reflexive pushbacks that happen in the first 5-10 seconds — before you've even gotten into Discovery. Agents often freeze here or jump straight into a full pitch, which makes it worse. The goal is a short, low-friction re-hook that buys you 15-20 more seconds, not a full rebuttal. If the re-hook fails, let them go gracefully — don't force it.

"I'm not interested."

"No worries at all — quick one though, is that 'not interested in cleaning services' in general, or just not interested in hearing from us again? If it's the first one, totally fine, I'll let you go. If it's more about us, the offer's actually changed a lot since we last reached out — worth 15 seconds?"

"I no longer need this / I don't need a cleaning service anymore."

"Got it, makes sense — situations change. Out of curiosity, was that because you found another solution, or just don't have the need right now? Either way, no pressure — just wanted to check before I update our records on your file."

"How did you get my number? / Why are you calling me?"

"Good question — you'd checked out home cleaning services on one of our sites a little while back, that's how we have this number. I know it's been a bit since then, which is exactly why I'm calling — wanted to see if it's still something you'd want now that pricing's changed. Bad timing, or got a sec?"

"Take me off your list / stop calling me."

"Understood, I'll make sure that's taken care of right away — you won't get another call from us. Have a good one."
(Tag the lead as Do Not Call in the CRM immediately. Do not attempt a re-hook on this one — this is a hard stop.)

"Is this a sales call?" (asked suspiciously, before you've said anything)

"Ha, I won't pretend it's not — but I'll keep it short. We've actually lowered our cleaning service pricing since you last looked into us, and I just wanted to make sure you saw that before I move on to my next call. Want the 20-second version, or should I let you go?"

Silence / one-word "yeah?" (guarded, unclear tone)

"Hey, no worries if this isn't a good time — I'll be quick either way. This is (Your Name) with Homeaglow, following up on a home cleaning quote you'd looked at before. Just checking if that's still something you'd want, since pricing's improved since then. Quick yes or no is totally fine."

General rule of thumb

  • One re-hook attempt only — don't stack multiple "just one more thing" asks
  • Always lead the re-hook with what's new, not a recap of the old pitch
  • If the objection is a hard DNC request, log it immediately and move on — do not re-hook

Phase 2: Discovery

The goal here is to find out why they didn't book the first time, and whether that reason still applies. Most recycled leads fall into one of three buckets: price was too high, timing was off, or they forgot/got busy. Identify which one fast so you can address it directly later.

Re-engagement Questions

Q1 — Re-open the file: "Just to refresh my memory on your end too — when you looked into this before, do you remember what made you hold off? Was it more the price, the timing, or just life getting busy?"

Q2 — Current situation: "And how's that been going for you since then — still handling the cleaning yourself, or did you end up going with someone else?"

Q3 — Logistics refresh: "Just to make sure I've got the right info — what area are you in, and roughly how many bedrooms and bathrooms are we talking about?"

If they say "I forgot about it" / "got busy"

"Totally get it — happens to everyone. Good news is, it'll take less than two minutes to get this sorted today, and I can walk you through pricing that's actually better than what we had before. Sound good?"

If they say "I went with someone else"

"That's great that you found someone! Out of curiosity, how's that been working out — anything you wish were different, like pricing, reliability, or flexibility? I only ask because a lot of people keep us as a backup option since we can usually slot in fast if their regular cleaner falls through."

What to capture

  • Original objection (price / timing / forgot / found alternative)
  • Bedrooms & bathrooms
  • Area / city-state (for availability)
  • Whether current need still exists

Phase 3: Elevator Pitch — What's Changed

Since these leads heard a version of Homeaglow before, don't repeat the full intro — frame this as "here's what's different now" to create a reason to re-engage.

"So here's the quick update — Homeaglow's now the largest home cleaning company in the country, over 2.6 million cleanings done for almost 900,000 customers, and we've actually lowered our rates since the last time we talked. We connect you with background-checked cleaners — over 20,000 of them, including a good number right in your area — and the pricing now starts a lot lower than before."

Shorter version (if they're impatient): "Long story short — bigger network, lower prices than when you last looked, and your first cleaning can be as low as $9. Want me to run the numbers for your place?"

Phase 4: Data Gathering

Quick confirmation of the basics so the pricing you give in the next phase is accurate and specific to them — specificity builds trust on a recycled lead.

"Just to make sure I give you the right numbers — how many bedrooms and bathrooms are we cleaning, and about how often were you thinking — once a month, every other week, or weekly?"

Checklist

  • Bedrooms & bathrooms
  • Desired frequency
  • Any pets / specific areas of focus

Once you have hours + frequency, proceed to Sales Pitch and use the DHJ Voucher calculator on the right to quote the numbers live.

Phase 5: Sales Pitch

We have three offerings. Lead with the DHJ Voucher + ForeverClean membership — it's our best value and the easiest to make sound like a no-brainer on a recycled lead. Use One-Time or Trial as fallback offers if they push back on membership.

Offering 1 — DHJ Voucher + ForeverClean Membership (lead with this)

"Okay, here's the good news — for a home your size, we'd recommend starting with a (X)-hour first cleaning. Normally that'd run you around (national average price), but since you checked us out before, I can actually get you that first cleaning for just (voucher price) — that's our 2-hour at $9, 3-hour at $19, 4-hour at $39, or 6-hour at $79, depending on what your place needs."

"That voucher comes with a ForeverClean membership at $59 a month, which is what unlocks the discounted rate of about $23/hour for every cleaning after that. Based on the (frequency) you mentioned, your future cleanings would run around (estimated monthly cost) — way below what most people pay locally."

Cancellation framing: "And just so you know upfront — you can cancel anytime. If you cancel before the 6-month minimum, the only thing you'd owe is a flat $35/hour based on your first cleaning's hours, so for your (X)-hour cleaning that'd be (X × $35)."

Offering 2 — One-Time Cleaning (fallback if they don't want a membership)

"No worries if a membership isn't what you're after — we also do one-time cleanings, no strings attached, at $63 an hour. So for a (X)-hour cleaning, that'd come out to (X × $63) total, one-time, done."

Offering 3 — Trial Cleaning (middle-ground option)

"If you want to try us out before committing to anything, we also have a trial cleaning option at $52.50 an hour — so a (X)-hour trial would be (X × $52.50). You'd have 30 days to decide if you want to continue with a membership or not, no pressure either way."

Negotiation — if they hesitate on the $59 membership fee

Step 1: "I hear you — totally fair. If the $59 is what's giving you pause, I can actually bring that down to $54 a month for you. Would that work better?"

Step 2 (if still hesitant): "I can go one more step and get that down to $49 a month — that's the lowest I'm able to go, and it still gets you the same $23/hour discounted rate going forward."

If the issue is the 6-month commitment: "If 6 months feels like a lot, I can shorten that to 5 or 4 months instead. In some cases I can even get it down to 2 months if that's what makes this work for you."

Phase 6: Booking

Move quickly from agreement to a scheduled date — momentum matters most on a recycled lead, since hesitation here often means another multi-week gap before they answer again.

"Awesome, let's get this locked in. I just need your card details to apply the voucher — are we using a credit or debit card? And can I grab your ZIP code so I can check cleaner availability in your area?"

"What date works best for your first cleaning? If you don't have an exact date yet, we can usually get someone out within 2 days — would (2 days from today) work, or would you rather set a standing appointment and adjust the date later?"

Phase 7: Booking Proper

Lock in the final details and confirm the terms clearly — this protects against early cancellations and chargebacks, which are more common on recycled leads who may be on the fence.

"Let's go over everything real quick. Your email is ___, phone number is ___, and address is ___ — are those correct? Your cleaning is booked for (date), for (X) hours, covered by your (voucher amount) voucher. Your priority areas are (___)."

"After this first cleaning, your ForeverClean membership starts and the first $59 (or negotiated rate) membership fee will be charged, renewing monthly until you cancel. Future cleanings will be around $23/hour. You can cancel anytime — if it's before the (6/5/4/2)-month minimum, the early termination fee is $35 multiplied by the hours of your first cleaning, which comes to (X × $35) in your case. Does all of that sound good?"

Verbatim

Exact phrasing for sensitive moments — read these as written.

Cancellation policy: "You can cancel or pause your membership anytime through your dashboard. If you cancel before completing the agreed minimum commitment, the early termination fee is a flat $35 per hour, based on the hours of your very first cleaning."

Membership renewal: "After your first appointment, the ForeverClean membership fee will be charged and will automatically renew each month at the agreed rate until you cancel."

Payment: "I'll need your card details now to apply the voucher and confirm the booking — you won't be charged anything beyond the voucher amount until after the cleaning is completed."

Value Statements

Use these to re-anchor value mid-call, especially with leads who are skeptical because "they've heard this before."

  • "We've actually lowered pricing since the last time you checked us out — your first cleaning is cheaper now than it would've been before."
  • "Every cleaner is background-checked, and 91.4% of jobs are completed by cleaners rated 4.5 stars or higher."
  • "With over 20,000 cleaners on the platform, over 90% of cleaning requests get picked up — and more than half within 50 minutes of booking."
  • "You're never locked in — cancel or pause anytime through your dashboard, no calls needed."
  • "Even if this isn't the right time for a membership, the one-time and trial options let you try us with zero long-term commitment."

Common Objections — Phoenix

These are written for recycled/aged leads specifically. The goal of each response: acknowledge without apologizing excessively, give them a reason "this time is different," and get to a small yes (price check, quick number) rather than a full re-pitch.

"I already said no to this before."

"Totally fair, and I appreciate you being upfront. The reason I'm circling back is genuinely because the pricing's changed since then — it's noticeably lower now. If price was the original holdup, would it be worth 30 seconds to hear the new numbers? If it's a flat no regardless, no worries, I won't keep you."

"I don't remember signing up for this / how did you get my number?"

"Good question — you'd looked into home cleaning services on one of our sites a little while back, which is how we have your info on file. I'm not trying to catch you off guard — just following up since it's been a few weeks. Would now be a bad time, or can I take 30 seconds?"

"It's too expensive" (still, after hearing new pricing)

"I hear you. Just so I'm not missing something — is it the membership fee that's the sticking point, or the hourly rate? Because I can actually flex on the membership side — I can bring it down to $54, and in some cases $49. Would that change things, or is it more that cleaning services in general aren't in the budget right now?"

"I'm not interested" (flat, no reason given)

"No problem at all. Can I ask — is that a 'not interested in cleaning services at all,' or more 'not interested in a membership specifically'? The reason I ask is we also do one-time cleanings with zero commitment, starting at $63/hour, if that's a better fit. If it's a hard no either way, I'll let you go — just wanted to check."

"I found someone else already."

"That's great, glad you got that sorted! Quick thought though — a lot of our customers actually keep us on file as a backup, since our network can usually get someone out within a day or two if their regular cleaner ever falls through or moves. Would it be worth grabbing the $9 trial just to have us as an option, no commitment attached?"

"Now's not a good time / I'm busy" (used to brush off the call)

"Totally understand — I'll be quick. Can I just ask one thing: if the timing were better, is this something you'd still want? If yes, I can text you the pricing right now so you're not stuck on the phone, and you can book whenever it's convenient. If it's a no regardless of timing, that's totally fine too — just let me know."

"Can you just send me the info / a link instead?"

"Sure thing, happy to. Real quick though — while I've got you, can I just confirm your bedroom/bathroom count so the pricing I send is actually accurate to your place, instead of a generic range? Takes 10 seconds and means the info is actually useful when you look at it later."

"This sounds like a scam / how do I know this is legit?"

"Fair to be cautious, especially with an unexpected call. We're Homeaglow — been around over 10 years, 2.6 million cleanings completed, every cleaner is background-checked. If it helps, I can send the booking confirmation to your email right now so you can see it's tied to a real account before any charge happens."

"I need to think about it / talk to my spouse."

"Of course, makes sense. While I have you — would it help if I hold one of the $9 promo vouchers on your file for the next 24 hours? That way if you both decide to go ahead, the pricing's already locked in and you're not starting from scratch. Want me to do that?"

Discovery Questions

Use these to quickly diagnose what kind of recycled lead you're talking to.

  • "When you first looked into this, what was the main thing that gave you pause?"
  • "Has anything changed on your end since then — new place, more time, different budget?"
  • "If price were a non-issue, is this something you'd want for your home?"
  • "Are you currently using any cleaning service, or handling it yourself?"
  • "What would make this an easy yes for you today?"

FAQs

Why is this lead being called again?

This is a recycled/aged lead (2-5+ weeks old) that didn't convert on the first attempt. Pricing or offer details may have changed since the original call, which is the basis for re-engagement.

What if the customer doesn't remember any prior interaction?

That's expected and common. Briefly explain they looked into cleaning services on one of our sites previously — don't over-explain or sound defensive about it.

How low can the membership fee go?

Standard is $59/month. It can be negotiated down to $54, and as a final step to $49 if that's still a blocker.

How flexible is the commitment term?

Standard minimum is 6 months. It can be reduced to 5 or 4 months, and in special cases down to 2 months if needed to close.

What's the difference between the three offerings?

DHJ Voucher + ForeverClean membership is the main recurring offer with the lowest ongoing rate ($23/hr) but has a commitment term and ETF. One-Time Cleaning ($63/hr) has zero commitment. Trial Cleaning ($52.50/hr) gives a 30-day window to decide before any membership starts.

What's the ETF if they cancel early?

$35 per hour, based on the hours of the first cleaning — applies only to the DHJ Voucher + membership offer.
